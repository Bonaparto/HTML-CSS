Almaty

Alma-Ata, or Almaty - is a city of republican importance and is the largest locality of Kazakhstan, former capital of Kazakhstan (until 1997), Kazakh SSR (as a part of USSR), Kazakh ASSR (as a part of RSFSR).

According to information from verified sources population of the city was 1916822 in the beginning of 2020.

Importance

From 1927 to 1936 Alma-Ata was the of Kazakh ASSR; from 1936 to 1991 - the capital of Kazakh SSR; from 1991 to 1997 - fist capital of Republic of Kazakhstan. Almaty was the largest city of Kazakh SSR and now is the largest of Republic of Kazakhstan.

Name

The government of Kazakhstan the city is called Almaty in kazakh and russian languages; During the dark ages in this region existed a camp for turk and mongol normads - Almaty, later discoverd by archaeologists;
1854 - Zailiyskoe strengthening was constructed on its place, after that - Verniy;

Climate

Climate in Almaty is continental and is characterised by an effect of mountain-valley circulation, which is noticed the most in Northern part of the city, located directly in the zone of transition from mountains to plain.
