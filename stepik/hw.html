Almaty

Alma-Ata, or Almaty - is a city of republican importance and is the largest locality of Kazakhstan, former capital of Kazakhstan (until 1997), Kazakh SSR (as a part of USSR), Kazakh ASSR (as a part of RSFSR).

According to information from verified sources population of the city was 1916822 in the beginning of 2020.

Importance

From 1927 to 1936 Alma-Ata was the of Kazakh ASSR; from 1936 to 1991 - the capital of Kazakh SSR; from 1991 to 1997 - fist capital of Republic of Kazakhstan. Almaty was the largest city of Kazakh SSR and now is the largest of Republic of Kazakhstan.

Geography

Climate

Climate in Almaty is continental and is characterised by an effect of mountain-valley circulation, which is noticed the most in Northern part of the city, located directly in the zone of transition from mountains to plain. Average year-to-year air temperature is 10 degrees Celsius, air temperature of the coldest month (January) is -4,7 degrees Celsius, the warmest (Jule) is +23,8 °C. Frosts usually begin on 14th of October, end on 18th of April. Weather above 30 degrees is averagely 36 days in a year.

Hydrography

Generally, Almaty is described with quite well-developed hydrographic network, consisting of natural rivers, water channels and reservoirs. There are several key factors of this: foothill location, significant amount of rainfall, melting of high alpine glaciers in summer, building canals. Big and Small Almatinka rivers flow through the city.

History

Dark ages

Basing on arheologic findings on territory of Almaty, it can be stated that this location has been inhabited by normads for a long time. Firstly, it was inhabited by saks. Saks were shifted by uisuns. After that the territory belonged to West-Turk khaganate, Turgesh and Karluk khaganates, also to Karahanid's state.
In VIII - X centuries on the territory of modern Almary existed multiple localities, one of which was probably called "Almatu" and was crossed by Silk Road.
At the beginning of XIII century the region was taken by mongols.
At the end of XVI century Almatu's territory decreased and only a small part of it was saved, where dulats from elder juz were living.

Verniy

On 4th of February, 1854, russian government made a decision to construct a military base on the left side of Small Almatinka. On 11th of April of 1867 Verniy became a center of Turkestan Government-General. In the beginning of 1913 the population count was more than 41 thousand, 59 industrial objects were build.
